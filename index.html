Research activity

I work on the development of lumped element microwave kinetic inductance detectors for the observation of the Cosmic Microwave Background radiation. I am mainly involved in two experiments: MISTRAL and COSMO.

Kinetic Inductance Detectors

A KID is superconducting resonant circuit whose kinetic inductance varies as a function of the optical energy absorbed. We measure the change of its resonant frequency as a response of a change in its kinetic inductance. See Mazin thesis for more.

MISTRAL

Millimetric Sardinia Radio Telescope reciever based on array of lumped element KIDs. MISTRAL is a cryogenic camera with an array of 415 KIDs cooled at 270mK that will be installed on the 64m diameter Sardina Radio Telescope and it will be sensitive to the 90GHz band. It will be a high resolution instrument with 13 arcseconds of angular resolution.

-

COSMO

Cosmic monopole observer. COSMO is an experiment aimed at measuring the monopole specral distortions of the cosmic microwave background radiation expectec at 1ppm. It will be a differential instrument based on a Martin-Puplett interferometer with two arrays of multimode KID sensitive to the 150GHz and 250GHz bands cooled at 150mK.

I also contribute to improve our KIDs readout electronics software. I'm familiar with the ROACH2 board, an FPGA based readout electronics.
