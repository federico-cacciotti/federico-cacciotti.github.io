Astrophotography

home page

Even though I haven't expensive and sophisticated equipments, I tried to do some photos with everything I have available.

I own an old Tanzutsu Skymaster telescope: a 114mm newtonian reflector, F1000, f/8.7 barlow (3x). I haven't a particularly suitable mount for astrophotography: it isn't motorised and it gets a little wobbly. Anyway, I decided to try to do astrophotography. After hours and hours of night observations without a properly motorised mount, I decided to build my own using an Arduino board and a stepper motor. I designed and 3D printed a geared system which allowed me to connect the stepper motor to the mount. I wrote a simple code to drive the motor with a particular speed and step angle and I connected also a bluetooth module in order to control this system with my smarphone, adjusting the motor speed when needed. You can see in the photo the old wooden mount with the Arduino module on the left leg, the stepper motor, my Canon reflex attached to the eypiece and also the 3D printed star finder on the telescope.

I own a Canon 600D reflex camera that I use for my astrophotography.

46P/Wirtanen comet

12/12/2018 - This is my first attempt in shooting photos to 46P/Wirtanen comet: it's the tiny halo at the center. At the shooting time it was about 4.5 mag. This image is a stack of 8x30sec ISO3200 + 22 dark/17 flat/23 bias with Canon EOS 600D + 18-55 Canon lens on my diy telescope motorised mount.
I also animated the same single shoots as the previous image. It shows a time interval of 23 minutes. Yes, It mooves!
16/12/2018 - Here I took some pictures with my Canon EOS 600D of the comet and Pleiades. It is a stack of 15x30 seconds ISO3200 exposures plus 25 darks and 20 biases and I did the pre-processing with Nebulosity on my MacBook PRO, it's a nice program, and after I used Photoshop to make the final editing and color balance.
